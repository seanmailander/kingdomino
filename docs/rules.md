# Rules for Kingdomino

## Introduction

You are a Lord seeking new lands to expand your Kingdom. You must explore all the lands, wheat fields, lakes, and mountains in order to spot the best plots. But be careful, as some other Lords are also coveting these lands.

Note: Before your first game, assemble the 4 small castles.

## Object of the Game

Connect your dominoes in order to build your kingdom in a 5x5 grid in a way that scores the most prestige points.

## Setup

Place the box as indicated in the original rules.

Each player takes:

- In a 2-player game: two kings of their color.
- In a 3- or 4-player game: one king of their color.

Each player also takes a starting tile and the castle of their color. They place the tile face up in front of them, then place the castle on it.

Shuffle all dominoes thoroughly with their numbers face up, then randomly place them in the box insert to form the draw pile.

- In a 2-player game: remove 24 dominoes at random and set them aside. Use the remaining 24.
- In a 3-player game: remove 12 dominoes at random. Use the remaining 36.
- In a 4-player game: use all 48 dominoes.

Then, from the draw pile, take the same number of dominoes as the number of kings in play:

- 3 dominoes for a 3-player game.
- 4 dominoes for a 2- or 4-player game.

Display them next to the box with their numbers visible, in ascending order, with the lowest number closest to the box. Then turn them to their landscape side.

One player takes all kings in hand, shuffles them thoroughly, and reveals them one at a time. When your king appears, place it on an empty domino in the line. Each domino can have only one king, so the last player has no choice.

In a 2-player game, each player chooses 2 dominoes, one for each king.

When all dominoes in the line have been chosen, form a new line the same way by drawing new dominoes.

## Playing the Game

The playing order is determined by the positions of the kings on the domino line.

The player whose king is on the 1st domino in the line, the one closest to the box, starts and takes the following actions:

1. Add the chosen domino to their territory according to the connection rules.
2. Choose a new domino in the new line by placing their king on it.

Then the player whose king is on the second domino takes their two actions, and so on until the last player has played.

In a 2-player game, each player performs these two actions twice, once for each of their kings.

After all players have acted, form a new domino line and start a new turn.

The game lasts:

- 12 turns in a 3- or 4-player game.
- 6 turns in a 2-player game, since each player acts twice each turn.

## Connection Rules

Players must build a kingdom in a 5x5 grid. Each domino is composed of two squares. To place a domino, a player must:

- Either connect it to the starting tile. The starting tile is considered wild, so any landscape may connect to it.
- Or connect it to another domino by matching at least one of its landscapes, horizontally or vertically only.

If you cannot add a domino to your kingdom following these rules, the domino must be discarded and scores no points.

All dominoes in your kingdom must fit within the 5x5 grid. If, due to poor planning, one or more dominoes cannot fit, they are discarded and score no points.

## End of the Game

When the last dominoes are lined up, players take one final turn, but they perform only action 1: placing the chosen domino.

Each player should end with a 5x5 kingdom in front of them, though some kingdoms may be incomplete if dominoes had to be discarded.

Then each player calculates prestige points as follows:

- A kingdom is made up of different properties: groups of connected squares of the same landscape type, connected horizontally or vertically.
- Each property scores prestige points equal to the number of squares in the property multiplied by the number of crowns in that property.
- You can have multiple properties of the same landscape type in the same kingdom.
- A property with no crowns scores 0 points.
- Each player adds together the points from all properties to get their final score.

The player with the highest score wins.

If there is a tie, the player with the largest property wins, whether that property contains crowns or not.

If there is still a tie, the player with the most crowns wins. If there is still a tie after that, the tied players share the victory.

## Additional Rules

### Dynasty

Play 3 rounds in a row. At the end of the 3 rounds, the player with the highest total score wins.

### The Middle Kingdom

Score 10 additional points if your castle is in the center of your kingdom.

### Harmony

Score 5 additional points if your territory is complete and no dominoes were discarded.

### The Mighty Duel

For 2 players. After one or two practice games, the real champions battle to build the largest kingdoms: use all the dominoes to build a 7x7 grid. A pen and paper may be useful to keep the final score.

You can mix these variants according to your preference.
